Recommended build approach

Launch as a PWA first, then package for iOS and Android.

For the fastest and most affordable MVP, WHSF can start with a Progressive Web App connected to the existing website. Later, the same experience can be packaged for Google Play and the Apple App Store.

FrontendResponsive PWA using the existing WHSF website style
BackendSupabase or Firebase for user profiles, roles and activity records
NotificationsFirebase Cloud Messaging or OneSignal for app alerts
GivingConnect the official PayPal charity donation link and future campaigns
LearningLink directly to WHSF e-Classroom, certificates and learner dashboards
PackagingUse Capacitor or Flutter later for native app-store release
Phase 1

App store options

Three launch paths for WHSF.

WHSF can begin with a low-cost app-like experience immediately, then move toward official store distribution when the product is ready.

Fastest

Progressive Web App

Launch first as a mobile web app that users can install from the browser. This is best for early testing, donor updates, volunteer coordination and member communication.

  • No app-store approval required
  • Works from the WHSF website domain
  • Best first step for MVP validation
Start PWA pilot →
Android

Google Play Store

Package the WHSF app for Android and publish through Google Play Console once login, privacy, notifications and content management are ready.

  • One-time Google Play registration fee
  • Android App Bundle / release signing workflow
  • Best for broad access across Africa and global Android users
Google Play setup →
iOS

Apple App Store

Release for iPhone through Apple Developer Program and App Store Connect after the app has a polished onboarding, privacy policy and support workflow.

  • Annual Apple Developer Program membership
  • TestFlight beta testing before public launch
  • Best for donors, partners and international supporters using iPhone

Production readiness checklist

What WHSF should prepare before public app-store launch.

  1. Create WHSF developer accounts for Google Play and Apple Developer.
  2. Prepare app name, icon, screenshots, description, privacy policy and support email.
  3. Confirm login roles: donor, volunteer, member, coordinator and admin.
  4. Test notifications, donation links, contact forms and e-Classroom links.
  5. Run a small pilot with WHSF volunteers, members and trusted donors.
  6. Collect feedback, fix usability issues, then publish to app stores.
